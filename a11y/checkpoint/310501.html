Overview

In order to reduce the complexity of text and lower the required reading level, you can implement the following:

  • Cover a single topic or subtopic in each paragraph.
  • Use the simplest sentence forms consistent with the purpose of the content. For example, the simplest sentence form for English consists of Subject-Verb-Object, as in John hit the ball or The Web site conforms to WCAG 2.1.
  • Use sentences that are no longer than 25 words.
  • Divide longer sentences into two where possible.
  • Use no more than two conjunctions per sentence.
  • Indicate logical relationships between phrases, sentences, paragraphs, or sections of the text.
  • Avoid professional jargon, slang, and other terms with a specialized meaning that may not be clear to people.
  • Replace long or unfamiliar words with shorter, more common terms.
  • Remove redundant words.
  • Use single nouns or short noun phrases.
  • Replace complex words or phrases with more commonly used words without changing the meaning of the sentence.
  • Use bulleted or numbered lists instead of paragraphs that contain long series of words or phrases separated by commas.
  • Make clear pronoun references and references to other points in the document.
  • Use the active voice, unless there is a specific reason for using passive voice. Sentences in the active voice are often shorter and easier to understand than those in the passive voice.
  • Use verb tenses consistently.
  • Use names and labels consistently.

Basic Requirements and Best Practices

= Requirement
= Best Practice

  • When text requires more than a 9th grade reading level to understand, supplemental content must be provided.
  • Use the clearest and simplest language that is appropriate.
User Story: As a user with a reading disability, I want a version of difficult text that is easier to read so that I can understand the content.
Success: If you were a user with a reading disability, when you encountered difficult text, you would have additional or alternate content that is easier to understand.

Testing Logic

Does the content, excluding any proper names and titles, contain any difficult text which requires more than a 9th grade reading level?

  • Yes; Is there supplemental content such as explanatory graphics, audio, or an alternate version of the text that is easier to read?
    • Yes; PASS
    • No; See {{ site.data.3105.3105[31050101].name }}.
  • No; PASS

Web Test Procedure

  1. Remove proper nouns and titles from the text.
  2. Evaluate the text for reading level.
  3. If the reading level is higher than 9th grade, verify that there is supplemental content to explain the text or an alternate version that is easier to read.
