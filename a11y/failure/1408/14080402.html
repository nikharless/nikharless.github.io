Expected Result

Paragraph spacing is adjustable to at least 1.5 times the line spacing.

Actual Result

Paragraph spacing is less than 1.5 times the line spacing and cannot be adjusted by the user.

Solution

  • Set the bottom margin to at least 1.5 times the line-height value, OR
  • Provide a mechanism to change the paragraph spacing

Examples

You can calculate the minimum paragraph margin based on the font size. For example:

  1. Get the minimum line-height value by calculating font size x 1.5:
    1em x 1.5 = 1.5em
    This is the line-height value.
  2. Get the total paragraph spacing by calculating line-height x 1.5:
    1.5em x 1.5 = 2.25em
    However, the last line of the paragraph will still have the line-height value, so that must be subtracted from the total.
  3. Get the minimum paragraph spacing by subtracting the line-height from the total paragraph spacing:
    2.25em - 1.5em = .75em
    This is the number used for the bottom margin.

Why It's Important

Users with certain cognitive disabilities have difficulty reading blocks of text with insufficient spacing between paragraphs.
