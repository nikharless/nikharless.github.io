Expected Result

Visible sections of a web page are marked up semantically as landmarks.

Actual Result

Visible sections of the web page are marked up as landmarks, but the landmarks are not used correctly.

Solution

  • Ensure valid tags or roles are used:
    • Header: <header> or role="banner"
    • Footer: <footer> or role="contentinfo"
    • Navigation: <nav> or role="navigation"
    • Main: <main> or role="main"
    • Complementary: <aside> or role="complementary"
  • Ensure regions are nested correctly:
    • The <header> and <footer> elements must reside at the top level. If they are nested, they are not recognized as header or footer regions.
  • Uniquely identify each instance of duplicate region types:
    • Use aria-label or aria-labelledby on <nav> regions so users can tell what kind of links they contain.

Example

<html>
    <header>
        <img src="logo.png" alt="Yoyodyne Home">
        <nav aria-label="main>
            ...
        </nav>
    </header>
    <main>
        ...
        <aside aria-labelledby="recent">
            <h2 id="recent">Recent Articles</h2>
        </aside>
    </main>
    <footer>
        © 2024 Yoyodyne Propulsion Systems
        <nav aria-label="footer">
            ...
        </nav>
    </footer>
</html>

Why It's Important

Page regions are visible at a glance for sighted users. Landmarks allow assistive technology users to have this same information and to easily navigate to different areas of the page.
