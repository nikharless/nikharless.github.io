Expected Result

Color is not used as the only visual means of distinguishing links from regular text.

Actual Result

Color is the only visual means of distinguishing links from regular text.

Solution

Provide a visual means of distinguishing links from regular text in addition to color.

Examples

Underline

The most universally understood indicator of links is underlining.

Sufficient Contrast and Underline on Hover/Focus

If contrast is used along with color to differentiate links, the contrast ratio must be at least 3:1 with regular text. (Keep in mind, the color also has to maintain 4.5:1 contrast with the background.) In addition, another indicator must be displayed on hover and focus.

Why It's Important

People who are color blind or have low vision need an alternate means besides color of getting the information.
