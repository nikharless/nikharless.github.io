Expected Result

Color is not used as the only means of distinguishing a visual element.

Actual Result

Color is used as the only means of distinguishing a visual element.

Solution

Provide a visual means of conveying the information in addition to color. Possible solutions include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Instead of using legends on charts and graphs, label the elements directly.
  • Use shapes and patterns in addition to colors to fill in elements.
  • Ensure sufficient contrast between each color. (This only works when there's a small number of elements.)

Examples

Bar Chart

Rather than relying on the color of the bars, each bar is labeled with text.

Pie Chart

Rather than relying on the color of the wedges, each wedge of this pie chart is labeled with text.

Test Question

In addition to colors, this test question is labeled with numbers corresponding to the answers.

Which is the right-angled triangle?



Source

Why It's Important

People who are color blind or have low vision need an alternate means besides color of getting the information.
