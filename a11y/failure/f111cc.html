WCAG | 1.1.1 Non-text Content | 1.1.1.c Complex Images (Text Alternative)

Expected Result

 Complex images have a long text alternative that describes all the information provided by the image.

Actual Result

 The complex image does not have a short text alternative.

Solution

 Add a long text alternative which describes all the information provided by the image using one of the following methods:
  • Place a long description directly on the page, and use id and aria-describedby to associate the description with the image, OR
  • Place a long description on a separate page. Add a longdesc attribute to the <img> tag and set it to the location of the long description. Place a visible link adjacent to the image as well for better accessibility, OR
  • Place the image inside a <figure> element. Wrap the long description in a <figcaption> element inside the <figure> element.

Examples

Adjacent Long Description

An oil painting of three Egyptian women in white dresses with six naked children in a Mediterranean landscape. The woman on the left is standing, holding a child on her shoulders, and another child stands in front of her pointing off the left side of the canvas. The woman in the center is also standing and holding a child on her shoulders while holding his hand on the top of her head. The woman on the right is sitting cross-legged on the ground breastfeeding a baby while 2 more children play behind her.

Why It's Important

Blind and visually impaired users need to have a long text alternative for complex images so that they get all the information conveyed visually by the image itself.
