Expected Result

The text alternative for the meaningful image conveys the same information as the image in context.

Actual Result

The meaningful image has a text alternative, but it does not convey the same information as the image in context.

Solution

Change the text alternative to text which sufficiently conveys the same information as the image in context.

Examples

The image in the following examples might literally be described as "an old photograph of a mustached man in a cap driving an old car with no roof or windows". However, that may not convey the information intended by its context.

In an article about old cars

<img src="itala.jpg" alt="1907 Itala motor car">

In an article about Prince Scipione Borghese

<img src="itala.jpg" alt="Borghese's chauffeur, Ettore Guizzardi">

Why It's Important

  • Blind/visually impaired: Users need to have a text alternative for meaningful images so that the images can be interpreted by assistive technology such as screen readers and braille devices.
  • Cognitively impaired: Users who have difficulty understanding images may benefit from having a text description.
