Expected Result

Audio that plays automatically for more than 3 seconds has either a mechanism to pause or stop the audio or a mechanism to control the volume independently from the overall system volume.

Actual Result

Audio that plays automatically for more than 3 seconds does not have a mechanism to pause, stop, mute, or control the volume.

Solution

  • Don't start the audio automatically, OR
  • Ensure the audio doesn't play for more than 3 seconds, OR
  • Provide a mechanism to pause or stop the audio, OR
  • Provide a mechanism to control the volume of the audio independently of the system volume.

Failing Example

Failing examples are not provided, because they would interfere with the passing examples.

Passing Examples

HTML <embed> with 2-second sound

This example passes because, while it automatically plays on page load, it lasts less than 3 seconds.

<embed src="horse.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">

Looped HTML <audio> with autoplay and controls

This audio autoplays and loops, but it provides controls to pause it and adjust the volume.

<audio controls autoplay loop>
    <source src="guitar.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
    Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

Not Applicable

  • Moving, blinking, scrolling, auto-updating content:

Disabilities Affected

  • Blindness/Visual Impairment: Screen reader users may have difficulty hearing the screen reader when other sounds are playing.
  • Cognitive Impairment: People with attention disorders may have difficulty focusing on the content when audio is playing.
