Expected Result

Text transcript must include important sounds.

Actual Result

The prerecorded audio-only content has a text transcript, but it doesn't include important sound effects.

Solution

Update the text transcript to include all important sound effects in the audio.

Passing Example

Transcript

This example from the Invisibilia podcast includes descriptions of significant sounds.

ALIX SPIEGEL, HOST:

The human animal averages 60 to 100 heartbeats a minute. To me, that’s like the Goldilocks heartbeat - not too fast, not too slow, just right - you know, as heartbeats go.

(SOUNDBITE OF HUMAN HEART BEATING)

SPIEGEL: So steady and reassuring.

(SOUNDBITE OF HUMAN HEART BEATING)

SPIEGEL: But then there’s the black-capped chickadee, which beats 480 times a minute - a tempo so fast just listening to it makes me feel nervous.

(SOUNDBITE OF BIRD HEART BEATING)

Why It's Important

  • Deaf/hard of hearing: People who are deaf or hard of hearing need to be able to access the same information provided in the audio.
  • Information processing: People who have the ability to hear use transcripts to more easily process the auditory information.
  • Non-native speakers: Transcripts help non-native speakers of the audio language understand the content.
  • Environment: Transcripts allow all users to access the auditory information in environments where sound can't be used or cannot be heard.
  • SEO: Transcripts allow search engines to catalog the information contained in audio.
