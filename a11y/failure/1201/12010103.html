Expected Result

Text transcript must identify speakers.

Actual Result

The prerecorded audio-only content has a text transcript, but it doesn't identify each speaker.

Solution

Update the text transcript to identify all the speakers in the audio.

Example

Transcript

In this example from the This American Life podcast, any time the speaker changes, the speaker is identified.

Ira Glass Which got her thinking. And when she got back to work, without much to do, she decided to make a poster of her own.

Jessica Lee Williamson I just wrote “cat found” above two pictures of possums that I had printed off the internet. One of the possums looks like he’s crouching, disturbed by an invasive camera lens. And then the other possum is hissing.

Why It's Important

Users who can hear the audio can differentiate speakers by voice.

  • Deaf/hard of hearing: People who are deaf or hard of hearing need to be able to access the same information provided in the audio.
  • Information processing: People who have the ability to hear use transcripts to more easily process the auditory information.
  • Non-native speakers: Transcripts help non-native speakers of the audio language understand the content.
  • Environment: Transcripts allow all users to access the auditory information in environments where sound can't be used or cannot be heard.
  • SEO: Transcripts allow search engines to catalog the information contained in audio.
