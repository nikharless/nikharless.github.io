WCAG | 1.1.1 Non-text Content | 1.1.1.i Text as Non-Text Content (Text Alternative)

Expected Result Text characters used to represent other characters have a text alternative describing their meaning.
Actual Result Text characters used to represent other characters do not have a text alternative describing their meaning.
Solution
  1. Enclose the text in a <span> element and give it role="figure".
  2. Add an aria-label attribute to the <span> element and set it to a text alternative which describes its meaning.
Example

Text Look-Alikes

<span role="figure" aria-label="cook">&#x03F2;&#x043E;&#x03BF;&#x006B;</span>

Leetspeak

<span role="figure" aria-label="Mark is a newbie.">
    <pre>|\/|4R|&lt; 15 /\ n00b</pre></span>
Why It's Important Without a text alternative, blind and visually impaired users would hear the actual text characters rather than what the author intended them to mean.
