Expected Result

If the labels for individual controls in a group of controls do not provide enough context to understand the purpose of the controls, the form element group has an accessible name.

Actual Result

The labels for individual controls in a group of controls do not provide enough context to understand the purpose of the controls, but the form element group does not have an accessible name.

Solution

Provide an accessible name for the form element group, using one of the following methods:

  • Use the <fieldset> element to group form controls, and place the accessible name in the legend element, OR
  • Use the aria-labelledby attribute on the element with role="group" or role="radiogroup" and set it to the id value of the visible label to function as the accessible name.

Failing Examples

Passing Examples

Fieldset and legend

Pizza Toppings 
<fieldset>
        <legend>Pizza Toppings</legend>
        <label><input type="checkbox"> Pepperoni</label>
        ...
    </fieldset>

Radiogroup role

Pancake Toppings



<p id="radio_group_label">Pancake Toppings</p>
    <div role="radiogroup" aria-labelledby="radio_group_label">
        <input type="radio" name="toppings" id="syrup">
        <label for="syrup">Maple syrup</label><br>
        <input type="radio" name="toppings" id="berries">
        <label for="berries">Blueberries</label><br>
        <input type="radio" name="toppings" id="cream">
        <label for="cream">Whipped cream</label>
    </div>

Group role

Contact Information






<p id="group_label">Contact Information</p>
    <div role="group" aria-labelledby="group_label">
        <label for="name">Name: </label>
        <input type="text" id="name">
        ...
    </div>

Not Applicable

The following items are not applicable to this failure:

  • No groups:
    No groups of related form elements on the page
  • Group label not required:
    Group label is not required to understand the full purpose of the individual controls

Disabilities Affected

  • Blindness/Vision Impairment: Programmatic names enable compatibility with assistive technology such as screen readers and screen magnifiers.
  • Motor Impairment: Programmatic names enable compatibility with assistive technology such as speech recognition software.
