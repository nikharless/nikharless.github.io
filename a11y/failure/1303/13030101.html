Expected Result

Instructions do not rely on visual characteristics such as shape, color, size, visual location, or orientation to be understood.

Actual Result

Instructions rely on visual characteristics to be understood.

Solution

Add an additional non-visual reference, such as a text label, that can be accessed by color blind and screen reader users.

Failing Example

Shape

The instructions refer to the shield graphic within the link, but the text alternative does not identify the image as a shield.

Click the shield above to participate in the Customer Protection Program.

<a href="#"><img src="shield.png" alt="Customer Protection Program"></a>

Passing Example

Shape

One possible solution is to include the word “shield” in the text alternative. This will identify the shape for screen reader users, as well as for users who have images turned off.

Click the shield above to participate in the Customer Protection Program.

<a href="#"><img src="shield.png" alt="Customer Protection Program shield"></a>

Other possible solutions include:

  • Combining the image and the text into a link with the text "Participate in the Customer Protection Program".
  • Changing the instructions to "Click the Customer Protection Program link above to participate," referring to the original alt text.

Why It's Important

  • Blind/visually impaired: Users may not be able to follow instructions if they rely on a visual description of the content.
  • Colorblind: Users may not be able to follow instructions if they rely on references to color.
  • Cognitively impaired: Users may benefit from additional text cues.

References
