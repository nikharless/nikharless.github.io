Expected Result

Adjacent parts of a graph required to understand the information have 3:1 contrast.

Actual Result

Adjacent parts of a graph required to understand the information do not have 3:1 contrast.

Solution

Change the colors of the graph so that parts required to understand the information have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Examples

Pie Chart

This pie chart has some wedges which have a contrast ratio of less than 3:1 with each other. However, a white line is visible between them which does have 3:1 contrast with the wedges on either side, so this passes.

Why It's Important

Color blind and visually impaired users need to be able to distinguish different parts of graphs required to understand the information it presents.
