Expected Result

Text characters used as graphic symbols have at least a 3:1 contrast with the background.

Actual Result

Text characters used as graphic symbols do not have at least a 3:1 contrast with the background.

Solution

Change the colors of the text characters to have at least 3:1 contrast with the background.

Examples

Close Button

Text characters such as x, <, and > are often used as graphic labels for buttons. Since these function as images rather than literal text, the non-text contrast rule of 3:1 applies.

Menu Button Using FontAwesome

FontAwesome allows you to use icon font characters as images, such as the down arrow on a drop-down menu. The non-text contrast rule of 3:1 applies to them as well.
