Expected Result

Visual indicators of a control's selected state must have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Actual Result

Visual indicators of a control's selected state do not have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Solution

Change the color of the selected state boundaries to have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Examples

Background

The background of the selected tab has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with the external background.

Screen Reader
Braille Device
Switch Device
Voice Recognition

Border

The border of the selected list item has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with the external background. The internal background is not required to distinguish the state, so it doesn't need to meet a contrast minimum.

Screen Readers:
  • JAWS
  • NVDA
  • VoiceOver
  • TalkBack
  • Orca

Why It's Important

Color blind and visually impaired users need to be able to distinguish the control in the selected state.
