Expected Result

Visual indicators of a control's focus state must have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Actual Result

Visual indicators of a control's focus state do not have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Solution

Change the color of the focus state boundaries to have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Examples

Border

The focus state border has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with both the internal and external background.

The following text field shows a cursor when focused. This cursor is the browser default and satisfies the checkpoint.

The focus state border has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with both the internal and external background. Since the internal background is not required to distinguish the focus state, it is not required to meet the contrast minimum.

Border and Background

This control's focus state has a border and background around the entire hit area, making it very clear and distinguishable. The focus state border has at least 3:1 contrast with the background, so the background color of the focus state is not required to distinguish the control and doesn't need to meet the contrast ratio.

I agree to the terms of use.

Internal Border

The focus indicator has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with the internal background.

Why It's Important

Color blind and visually impaired users need to be able to distinguish which control is in focus.
