Expected Result

Visual indicators of a control's hover state must have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Actual Result

Visual indicators of a control's hover state do not have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Solution

Change the color of the hover state boundaries to have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Examples

Border

The hover state border has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with both the internal and external background.

Border

The hover state border has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with the external background. Since the internal background is not required to distinguish the hover state, it is not required to meet the contrast minimum.

Border and Background

This control's hover state has a border and background around the entire hit area, making it very clear and distinguishable. The hover state border has at least 3:1 contrast with the background, so the background color of the hover state is not required to distinguish the control and doesn't need to meet the contrast ratio.

I agree to the terms of use.

Background

The hover state has a contrast ratio of at least 3:1 with the background. It is not necessary for the hover state to contrast with the default state.

Why It's Important

Color blind and visually impaired users need to be able to distinguish the control in the hover state.
