Expected Result

Parts of graphic required to understand its meaning have 3:1 contrast against adjacent colors.

Actual Result

Part of graphic required to understand its meaning does not have 3:1 contrast against adjacent colors.

Solution

Change the colors of the graphic so that parts required to understand its meaning have at least 3:1 contrast with adjacent colors.

Examples

Multi-color Icon

The hand in this icon has more than one color. However, the shape of the hand is understandable without contrast between those colors. The contrast between the hand and the background is sufficient, so the icon passes.

Why It's Important

Color blind and visually impaired users need to be able to distinguish and understand meaningful icons.
