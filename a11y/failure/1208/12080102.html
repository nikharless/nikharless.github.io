Expected Result

Prerecorded synchronized media and video-only media must have a text (transcript) alternative that includes all of the information presented in the visual content.

Actual Result

The media has a text transcript, but it is missing important information.

Solution

Update the text transcript to include all of the information presented in the video. Descriptions should be neutral, clear, and present tense.

Important visual information may include:

  • Actions
  • Facial expressions
  • Indication of dreams or memories
  • Passage of time
  • Scene changes
  • Text embedded in the video

Example

Text Transcript

This transcript does include the text on the cartoon images, which is positive. However, it should also include a brief description of the cartoons themselves to give context to the text.

TED: Liana Finck: Life is Hard. Art Helps.

Why It's Important

  • Blind/vision impaired: People who are blind or vision impaired need to be able to access the same information provided in the video track.
  • Information processing: People who have the ability to see use alternatives to more easily process the visual information.
  • Deafblind: Transcripts allow deafblind users access to information presented in video content through braille devices.
  • SEO: Transcripts allow search engines to catalog the information contained in video content.
