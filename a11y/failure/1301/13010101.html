Expected Result

Visible sections of a web page are marked up semantically as landmarks.

Actual Result

Visible sections of the web page are not marked up semantically as landmarks.

Solution

Mark up visible page regions as landmarks:

  • Header: <header> or role="banner"
  • Footer: <footer> or role="contentinfo"
  • Navigation: <nav> or role="navigation"
  • Main: <main> or role="main"
  • Complementary: <aside> or role="complementary"

Example

<html>
    <header>
        <img src="logo.png" alt="Yoyodyne Home">
        <nav aria-label="main>
            ...
        </nav>
        <form aria-label="site search"> ... </form>
    </header>
    <main>
        ...
        <aside aria-labelledby="recent">
            <h2 id="recent">Recent Articles</h2>
        </aside>
    </main>
    <footer>
        © 2024 Yoyodyne Propulsion Systems
        <nav aria-label="footer">
            ...
        </nav>
    </footer>
</html>

Why It's Important

Page regions are visible at a glance for sighted users. Landmarks allow assistive technology users to have this same information and to easily navigate to different areas of the page.
