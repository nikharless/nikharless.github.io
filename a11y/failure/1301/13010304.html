Expected Result

If the label for an individual control in a group of controls does not provide enough context to understand the purpose of the control, the control is programmatically associated with the group label.

Actual Result

The label for an individual option in a group of options in a selection list does not provide enough context to understand the purpose of the option, and the option is not programmatically associated with the group label.

Solution

Use the <optgroup> element to group the options.

  • Enclose the entire group of options in an <optgroup> element.
  • Place the group label in a label attribute in the <optgroup> element.

Example

<label for="pancakeToppings">Pancake Toppings</label>
<select id="pancakeToppings" name="pancakeToppings">
    <optgroup label="Fruits">
        <option value="1">Apples</option>
        <option value="2">Bananas<option>
        <option value="3">Blueberries<option>
    </optgroup>
    <optgroup label="Syrups">
        <option value="4">Maple<option>
        <option value="5">Blueberry<option>
        <option value="6">Strawberry<option>
    </optgroup>
    <optgroup label="Extras">
        <option value="7">Cinnamon</option>
        <option value="8">Pecans<option>
        <option value="9">Whipped Cream<option>
    </optgroup>
</select>

Why It's Important

Assistive technology users browsing through a form will not hear group labels unless they are programmatically associated. Screen readers in forms mode will ignore any text that is not programmatically associated with form controls.
